LESSON 28:Class 8 (continued)

Vocabulary

  • ubushe - burn (no pl.)
  • ubukene - need, poverty (no pl.)
  • ubgana - childhood (no pl.)
  • ubusore - youth (in sense of age, not person)
  • ubgoya - hair (except of human head) (no pl.)
  • uburyo - opportunity, way, kind (no pl.)
  • ubutumwa - message (no pl.) (when used with bgiza means gospel)
  • ubufu - flour (no pl.)

54. Observe the words in this class: ubgana (from umwana) and ubusore (from umusore). Many others are fonned this way.

  • e.g. Ubukristo (from Umukristo) meaning Christianity.

55. Another use of this class is in temporal expressions, such as:

  • bgakeye - good morning (that is not a literal translation)
  • burije - it is getting dark, it is late (in the day) (bgije in a dependent clause)
  • bukeye - the next day
  • ubu - now

You will learn other expressions from time to time involving this use.

56. Imperative of "to come". Kuza has no imperative in the singular, and thus you must use: ngo or ngwino - come, come here.

Exercises:

I. Translate into English:

  1. Ngwino n'ingoga; burije.
  2. Ubukene bg'umworo ni bginshi.
  3. Umwana wiwe afise ubushe bubi mu mutwe.
  4. Indwara y'umuhungu ni mbi cane.
  5. Genda ubu gufasha umwigisha gukora mu murima wiwe.
  6. Imana irafasha abantu mu bukene bgabo.
  7. Dusoma Ubutumwa Bgizoa mu Gitabo c'Imana.
  8. Abigishwa bagenda i muhira ubu kuko bgije.
  9. Bgakeye, n'amaki? N'amahoro.
  10. Akayabo gafise ubgoya bgiza bginshi.

II. Translate into Kirundi:

  1. We have a good opportunity (insert "bgo") to worship God.
  2. There is much joy in the Gospel of Jesus.
  3. Sheep's hair is long, but a dog's hair is short.
  4. We have great (much) need in our hearts; but Jesus is our savior and He has great mercy.
  5. We want an opportunity (insert "bgo") to help the poor people because they have great need.
  6. A woman is bringing (some) flour into the house.
  7. Put (some) oil on the child's burn.
  8. In my childhood I liked (na-kunda) to help my teacher.
  9. Come here; bring your (s.) peas and corn (pl.).
  10. Don't you want to read in the Gospel of John (Yohana)?
