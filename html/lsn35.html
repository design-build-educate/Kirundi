LESSON 35: Vowel-Stem Verbs

Vocabulary

  • kwādika - to write
  • kwīzera - to trust, believe
  • kwēreka - to show
  • kwīgisha - to teach
  • kwīgishwa - to learn, be taught
  • kwīhana - to confess, repent

69. Theese verbs have stems which begin with a vowel which is always long: -āndika, -īzera, etc. S1nce the vowel "u" before another vowel becomes "w" the infinitive ku-andika becomes kwandika. So whenever you see an infinitive beginning with "kw" you know it is a vowel-stem verb.

70. For the conjugation of these verbs it is very important to remember the rules for vowel and consonant changes. (par. 3, 14, 21, 22, 30, 39, 46, 50, 62.)

  • Here is the -ra present conjugation:

    ndizera - I trust turizera - we trust
    urizera - you trust murizera - you trust
    arizera - he trusts batizera - they trust
    Note: For other classes, the same principle applies, for it is the a of -ra that makes the contraction. In all forms of these vowel-stem verbs the accent falls on that first vowel of the stem, which is always long, thus: arāndika.

  • The prefixless present:

    nizera twizera
    wizera mwizera
    yizera bizera
    (Note this tense for the other classes:)
    Class 2 wereka yereka
    Class 3 yereka zereka
    Class 4 cereka vyereka
    Class 5 ryereka yereka
    Class 6 rwereka zereka
    Class 7 kereka twereka
    Class 8 bgereka yereka
    Class 9 kwereka yereka
    Class 10 hereka
  • The continuous present: ndiko ndigisha, urik' urigisha, etc.

71. Note in the 3rd person singular yizera, the vowel change of a. Instead of the vowel "a" dropping out before another vowel as you have learned, it here changes to "y". Thus, a-izera becomes yizera: likewise, yandika, yereka.

Exercises:

I. translate into English
  1. Umuntu (insert "who") yizera Imana azoronka umugisha.
  2. Abana bigishwa neza mw ishuli.
  3. Umukobga arikw arihana ivyaha vyiwe.
  4. Umwigisha yigisha abana benshi gusoma no kwandika. (No is the same as na, explanation later.)
  5. Ngomba kwereka abigishwa igitabo c'Imana.
  6. Unwigisha arikw arafasha abana kwandika.
  7. Abana bariko barereka abavyeyi babo impuzu zabo.
  8. Umuhungu w'umutama yigishwa n'ingoga indirimbo nsha.
  9. Igitabo c'Imana cigisha abantu urukundo n'ukwizera.
  10. Ntitugomba kugenda ubu kuko turiko turandika ivyete.

  1. The boys write well but they do not read well.
  2. The bad man is confessing his sins; he will receive the blessing of God and joy in his heart.
  3. I belleve the Word of God and I want to teach many people to trust God.
  4. Our Savior, Jesus, will save bad people. They will receive new hearts.
  5. His repentance is good.
  6. Do you (s.) trust the Savior? He wants to show people His love.
  7. The love of God shows people the good way.
  8. Do you (pl.) write letters in school?
  9. Parents teach their children to do well.
  10. We are learning to sing (some) nice new songs.
