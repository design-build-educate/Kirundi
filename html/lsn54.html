LESSON 54: Ordinary Past (continued)

In this lesson, instead of a new vocabulary, continue memorizing the past suffixes of verbs given in Lesson 53.

110. For forming this past tense in agreement with nouns of classes other than first, it is important to remember the rules for vowel and consonant change. Here are some examples:

Class singular plural
2nd wagiye yagiye
3rd yagiye zagiye
4th cagiye vyagiye
5th ryagiye yagiye
6th rwagiye zagiye
7th kagiye twagiye
8th bgagiye yagiye
9th kwagiye yagiye

111. For the conjugation of vowel-stem verbs in the ordinary past, remenber that 'a' before other vowels drops out.

  • nibagiye - I forgot
  • bemeye - they agreed
  • mwanditse - you (pl.) wrote
  • yubatse - he built
  • twogeje - we washed

Exercises:

I.Translate into English
  1. Inka zagiye ku ruzi kunywa amazd.
  2. Twibagiye kusana ibitabo vyacu.
  3. Mbega mwogeje neza amasahane yose?
  4. Twizeye Ijambo rya Yesu kuko twumvise ibitangaza (which) yakoze.
  5. Ubukene bg'umugabo bgazanye amarushwa menshi n'ubgoba.
  6. Impene yanyoye amazi mu ruzi.
  7. Ibikoko vyishe intama zitatu.
  8. Inkumi zogeje amasahane mabi yose.
  9. Akayabo kacu gafashe imbeba nyinshi.
  10. Umucanwa wazanye umwotsi mu nzu yose.
II. Translate into Kirundi
  1. Two blind men went to the church to pray.
  2. Thieves entered the house in the night because the man was sleeping hard (cane).
  3. The king's workmen built a big house in his kraal.
  4. I wrote the names of all the girls in the school.
  5. The new books fell on the floor.
  6. The old man died yesterday.
  7. Did yon (pl.) hear the words of the teacher?
  8. Did you (s.) remember to send three men to Gitega to buy food?
  9. Sin brought death to all people.
  10. We repented of all our sins and Jesus came into our hearts.
