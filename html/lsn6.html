LESSON 6: Possessive Particle

Vocabulary

  • umushatsi - hair (of the human head) (rarely used in plural)
  • umugozi - string, rope
  • umugisha - blessing
  • umurima -garden
  • ndakora - I am working, I work
  • ndagenda - I am going, I go
  • mfise - I have

12. Possessive particle. This is a small word used to express possession as exepressed in English by "of" or 's. It is always the same as the prefix of the possessive adjective. Thus

Class 1 sing.: wa plural: ba
Class 2 sing.: wa plural: ya

The possessive particle always follows the name of the thing possessed and agrees with it, not with the owner.

  • Umutima wa Yesu - the heart of Jesus (Jesus' heart)
  • Abana b'umwigisha - the teacher's children

(We will not give the possessive particle with each class. Simply remember that it is the same as the prefix of the possessive adjective.)

Note: The -a of the possessive particle elides before a following vowel, thus: w' , b' , etc., as seen in some of the preceding examples.
