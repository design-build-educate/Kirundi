LESSON 64: Stative Voice

Vocabulary

  • kunezera (nezerewe) - to be happy
  • kuwara (ye) to be sick, ill
  • gukomera (ye) - to be well, strong
  • gusonza (shonje) to be hungry
  • gukanya (nye) to be cold, wet

130. In Kirundi, words that express a condition or state of being, or bodily or mental attitude, are used in the stative voice -- which means that the prefix is -ra present, but the suffix is past. This applies to present time. For past and future time these verbs are formed like any others. In the negative and in dependent clauses the -ra drops out.

  • One would not say, Ndarwara - for, I am sick,
  • but, Ndarwaye.

Likewise,

  • I am happy. is, Ndanezerewe.
  • Ndicaye - I am sitting down.
  • Ndemeye - I am willing.
  • Ndizeye - I trust.

For habitual present of these verbs the regular -ra present is used. e.g.

  • Ndarwara imisi yosa - I am sick every day (or, always).

Exercises:

I.Translate into English

  1. Ndanezerewe cane kuko Yesu yampariye ivyaha vyanje kandi yanyogeje umutima.

  2. Umwana w'impumyi ararwaye cane. Baramujana ku muganga (or, lora muganga).

  3. Ntitwariye uyu musi; turashonje cane.

  4. Cana umuriro mwinshi; ndakanye.

  5. Umuhungu yarwaye mu gitondo arik' ubu arakomeye.

  6. Abana baranezerewe kuko baronse impuzu nshasha.

  7. Abigishwa baremeye gufasha abavyeyi babo mu mirima.

  8. Inzira iragoye, ariko ngira ngo turashika vuba.

  9. Inzu yacu nshasha irakomeye cane kandi ni nini.

  10. Umunyavyaha arizeye Yesu ; rer' (so) arakizwa ivyaha vyiwe.

II. Translate into Kirundi

  1. The children want a lot of food. They are very hungry. (a lot of - much)

  2. Two girls were absent today because they are sick.

  3. I trust Jesus because He saved me.

  4. Good morning; are you well? Yes, I am very well and I have peace in my heart.

  5. The teachers are willing to work hard to help their people to be saved.

  6. The shepherd's dog is dying; the doctor can't treat it.

  7. The clothes are wet; I'11 put them out in the sun. Remember to bring them in (this) evening.

  8. My rope is very strong. I bought it yesterday.

  9. Mary's baby (infant) is always sick. The doctor is treating it.

  10. We are very happy to see you (pl.). We haven't seen you (for) years.
