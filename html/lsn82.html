LESSON 82: Gurtya, Gurtyo

Vocabulary

  • umwete - zeal, enthusiasm
  • kugira umwete - to be zeaLous, be enthusiastic. energetic
  • kwaka (tse) - to shine
  • kunebga (bge) - to be lazy
  • gukekeranya (nye) - to doubt

163. Gurtya, gurtyo. These adverbs mean: gurtya - like this, gurtyo like that. This is one of the most difficult sounds in Kirundi. Practice with an African until you can say it well. These adverbs are variable, that is, they must take the same prefix as the verbs they modify.

  • yabikoze artya - he did it like this
  • babikoze bartyo - they did it like that
  • tugire durtya - let us do like this

When following an infinitive it takes 'gu' in agreanent with the infinitive. e.g.

  • tugomba kuririmba gurtyo - we want to sing like that

With the imperative it may be:

  • Kora urtya or Kora gurtya - do like this (s.):
  • but the polite form is always ukore urtya, mukore murtya - do like this.
Note:
  1. The absence of the r in the 1st pers. sing. -- Ndakora ntya - I do like this.
  2. The change-down rule is applied in this word, probably because the t sound is more predominant than the r.
  3. Do not try to use this form for "Do like John".

A common error of beginners is to want to always use gurtya or gurtyo, but it is better to make it agree with the verb it modifies.

Exercises:

I.Translate into English

  1. Paulo yagize umwete mwinshi mu bikorwa vy'Imana; natwe (and we) ni tugire durtyo.

  2. Kuk' umuco w'Imana waka urtyo tugende neza muri wo (it, i.e. light).

  3. Kuko mwinginze murtyo nsobibaha.

  4. Abahungu bararima neza; mwebge ni mukore murtyo.

  5. Toma ntiyizeye kugeza. aho (until) yabonye Yesu; ntidukekeranye durtyo.

  6. Imbga zirarya zirtya arikw abantu bararya bartya.

  7. Namb' (if) inkumi ibesha irtyo, ntidushobora kuyizera.

  8. Sinshobora guha umufundi akazi kukw anebge artyo.

  9. Umwana wacu ararwara artya imisi yose; mbega ntimushobora kumukiza?

  10. Akayabo kivye inyama gartyo; singomba ko kagaruka mu nzu.

II. Translate into Kirundi

  1. Let us be zealous to sing like this.

  2. (s.) Plane the boards like that.

  3. It is not nice to laugh like that.

  4. The children run like this.

  5. I did it like this, but you (pl.) do it like that.

  6. It's difficult to catch wild animals because they fear people like that.

  7. Do you (s.) write like this with a pencil?

  8. Stop laughing like that and help your sister (speaking to a boy).

  9. Because God's love is strong like that, He wants to save everybody.

  10. We can't bring in (from sun) the clothes because they are wet like that.
