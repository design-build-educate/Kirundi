SOME ORTHOGRAPHY RULES

(These rules were drawn up by an orthography committee of the Alliance.)

  1. Apostrophes should be used in these words when they are followed by a vowel:

    1. Word Example
      na N'abandi
      ni N'umwigisha
      si S'umuhungu
      -ri Ar' i muhira
      -ti At' Uzoza ryari?
      yamara Yamar' abana...
      ingene Ingen' abikoze
      ariko Arik' umuntu wese
      kubga Kubg' ivyo
      maze Maz' aragenda
      mbega Mbeg' uzoza?
      nka Nk'abana
      canke Cank' ibiti
      none Non' ubu
      kandi Kand' abantu...
      rero Rer' umuntu wese
      mbere Mber' aragiye
      erega Ereg' ivyiwe wyaheze
      ehe Eh' ari hano
      buno Bun' aragenda
      kuko Kuk' utabigomba
      yuko Yuk' uwagiye...
      ko K'ubivuze
      nuko Nuk' umuntu wese...
      ngo Ng' ubibone
      uko Uk' ugomba
      naho Nah' ukora iki
      iyo (as conjunction) Iy' agenda
      muri Mur' iyo nzu
      kuri Kur' uyo
      namba Namb' abikora
      asangwa Asangw' ugomba...
      hako Hak' ubiheba
    2. Kuko, ko, yuko, nuko, ngo, uko: the o changes to w before a, e, i. It is replaced by an apostrophe before u. Examples
      • kukw afise.
      • Yukw iri hano.
      • Kuk' uzogenda.
    3. N'ingoga has an apostrophe (not ningoga). Bikore n'ingoga.
    4. N'iki has an apostrophe in expressions like: N'iki gituma (as everywhere).
    5. Aho, iyo: the o is replaced by an apostrophe before all vowels:
      • Ah' uzozira.
      • Ah' ashaka
      • Iy' ataje.
    6. Uz' ugende, Az' abimenye. (This is a future imperative or subjunctive, with -za used as an auxiliary verb.)
    7. The possessive particle elides.

  2. Apostrophes in pronouns:

    1. An apostrophe is not used in personal, demonstrative, numeral, quantitative, interrogative pronouns. Examples

      • Ni we azogenda (not, Ni w'azogenda).
      • Uyo arakora (not, Uy' arakora).
      • Nta co ukoze (not, Nta c' ukoze).
      • Iyi irarwaye (not, Iy' irarwaye).
      • Har' ico ubona? (not, Har' ic' ubona?)
      • N'ico uyu akoze kizovugwa. (not, N'ic' uy' akoze...)
      • Batangazwa n'ivyo abishura. (not, Ivy' abishura).
      • Ni nde azogenda? (not, Ni nd' azogenda?)

    2. Pronouns that may have an apostrophe before a vowel:

      1. The possessive pronoun:
        • ivy' abantu (for ivya abantu),
        • iz' abakuru,
        • ab' iki gihe,
        • e.g. Kuk' ubgami bg'Imana ar' ubg' abameze bartyo.
        2. The demonstrative pronoun will have an apostrophe in these expressions:
        • ic' ari co,
        • uw' ari we,
        • ivy' ari vyo.
        • e.g. Sinzi uwo muntu uw' ari we.

  3. Words that must not have an apostrophe:

    1. All nouns, verbs (except those indicated in 1 a) and f), adjectives, adverbs, such as cane, ubu, nyene (except adverbs listed in no. 1 above).
    2. Nguyo. e.g. Nguyo Umwagazi w'intama. Ngiki igitabo (note that there is no apostrophe in the middle or the end of the word, and that the initial vowel of the word following is not removed.)
    3. Kurubu (when it means perhaps) e.g. Kurubu yaje, simbizi. (But if ubu is a separate word referring to time, the apostrophe is used: kur' ubu.)
    4. Before proper nouns none of these words elides, except words like: Imana, Abarundi, Abanyarwanda, etc. The reason for this exception is that Africans do not think of these words as proper nouns.

  4. Nuko and n'uko

    1. When it stands for ni uko, it is written n'uko: igituma atabikoze n'ukw atabimenye - the reason he didn't do it is that he didn't knov.
    2. When it stands for na uko, it is written n'uko: Vyatewe n'uko ntariho. - It was caused by (the fact) that I wasn't here.
    3. Nuko meaning thus, so, therefore, does not have an apostrophe. e.g.
      • Nuko rer' umuntu wese iy' ari muri Kristo... - Therefore every one who is in Christ...

  5. Suffixes: -ho, -ko, -yo, mwo, -na.

    1. -ko.
      • Before a and i it changes to kw: Yashizekw inkwi.
      • Before u it changes to k' : Yashizek' umunyu.
    2. -ho.
      • It changes to h': Harih' ibitabo. Harih' abantu. Harih' umunyu.
      • (In some areas people make the 0 a w before a and i, but we do not write it that way.)
    3. -yo. It changes to y' : Yashubijey' ibintu.
    4. -mwo. It changes to mw' : Yashizemw' ibintu.
    5. -na. An apostrophe is never used in this suffix. Baravugana ibikorwa

  6. Note the orthography of the following words:

    1. nta bgo, nta ho. These are written as two words (not, ntabgo), and do not take an apostrophe.
    2. ba se, ba nyina, ba Bgana, ba naka, ba nde, etc. (Two words in each case, )
    3. bene Data (two words, with a capita1 letter only on Data).
    4. Ntibaribgashike (this is the never-yet tense, and it is written as one word)
    5. amafaranga (not amafranga), Afirika (not Afrika), Igifaransa (not Igifransa).
    6. These words have pf: gupfa, gupfungana, urupfunguzo , impfunguzo, gupfunga , umupfuko, impfizi, gupfuka, gupfuma, ifpa, ipfupfu, amapfa, gupfinda.
    7. These words have only f (not pf): kwifuza, ifuku, ifuhe, gufukira (i.e. guhambagira), gufudika.

  7. Use of capital letters:

    1. Words referring to God are not capitalized, as: yo. yera. etc., except actual names of God: Imana, Rurema, Indavyi.
      • Names referring to people in a general sense are not capitalized: abera, abazungu, abirabura.
      • But names of tribes or races are capitalized: Abarundi, Ababiligi, Abanyafirika, etc.
    3. Igitabo c'Imana. This is capitalized since it refers to the Bible.
  8. You know that mu and ku change to muri and kuri before proper names. When these names begin with a vowel muri and kuri do not elide:
    • muri Amerika
    • muri Afirika
    • muri Efeso
    • muri Egiputa
  9. Quotation marks. Ordinarily, since -ti serves almost as quotation marks, the latter are not necessary when -ti is present (or, ngo). However, if there is ambiguity as to where the quotation ends, quotation marks may be used as well as -ti. e.g.
    • Bat' "Abantu baje." baraheza baragende - They said, "The people have come." then they went away.
    • Ni twemere ivyo Yesu yavuze, ati "Ni muze kuri jewe", twemere gukizwa na we. - Let's accept what Jesus said, "Come unto me", and let us be saved by Him.

  10. Words like the following have a y between the i and a: Mariya, Nehemiya, Eliya, etc.

  11. These French words are Kirundi-ized as follows:
    • commune - ikomine
    • million - imiliyoni
    • litre - ilitiri
    • kilometre - ikilometero (pl. ibi-)
    • L'etat - Leta
    • Amoriquo - Amerika
    • station - istasiyo
    • mission - imisiyoni
    • missionnaire - umumisiyoneri
    • gramme - igarame (pl. ama-)
    • Protestants - Abaprotestanti
    • Premier ministre - Ministre mukuru
    • Angleterre - Ubgongereza
