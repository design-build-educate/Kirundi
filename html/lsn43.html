LESSON 43: -ndi (Other)

Vocabulary

  • igihe - time, at the time when
  • igikomere - ulcer
  • amarushwa - troubles
  • umugenzi - friend (see par. 93)
  • kurondera - to look for, search for, to get
  • guhemba - to pay (for work done, not pay for an article)
Note: kurondera is used for "to get" only in the sense of "go to get". e.g. Genga kurondera umunyu - go get some salt.

92. -ndi, another, other. This word has for its prefix the characteristic letter of the class with an initial vowel. This adjective differs from the others learned thus far, in that it precedes the noun it modifies. Here it is given with a noun of each class, singular and plural:

Class 1 s. uwundi mugago another man
pl. abandi bantu other people
Class 2 s. uwundi murima another garden
pl. iyindi migozi other strings
Class 3 s. iyindi nka another cow
pl. izindi mbuto other seeds
Class 4 s. ikindi gitabo another book
pl. ibindi bihugu other countries
Class 5 s. irindi shuli another school
pl. ayandi majambo other words
Class 6 s. urundi ruzi another river
pl. izindi mbaho other slates (boards)
Class 7 s. akandi kayabo another cat
pl. utundi dukoko other insects
Class 8 s. ubundi bgato another boat
pl. ayandi mariri other beds
Class 9 s. ukundi kuboko the other arm
pl. ayandi meza other months
Class 10 s. ahandi hantu another place
(ahandi by itself means "elsewhere")
Notice that this adjective causes the initial vowel of the noun following to be dropped. Thus it is not abandi abantu, but abandi bantu - other people. Note: This word can also be used for "more". e.g. Mfise ibiti bitatu yamara ngomba ibindi bibiri -I have three trees but I want two more.

Exercises:

I.Translate into English
  1. Haza abandi bagabo batandatu.
  2. Abahungu bose ntibafise imbaho. Tugomba izindi cumi na zitatu.
  3. Unwigeme afise ibikomere bibiri ku kuguru n'ikindi ku kuboko.
  4. Genda lkurondera ibindi bijumbu vyinshi.
  5. Nzohemba abanyakazi uwundi musi; s'uyu musi.
  6. Abagenzi bacu bafise amarushwa menshi; ntibashobora gushika hano vuba; bazoza uwundi musi.
  7. Amazi yo (do not translate yo) mu ruzi ni make: tuzogenda kwoga ahandi.
  8. Petero azorondera akazi ahandi, uwundi mwaka.
  9. Sinshobora kuza i wanyu uyu musi; mugabo nzoza ikindi gihe.
  10. Muzogura ayandi magi ryari?
II. Translate into Kirundi
  1. The poor man has few francs and he has many other troubles.
  2. We will help the girls another time.
  3. A friend likes to help other people (at) all times.
  4. The teacher is looking for many more pupiLs.
  5. We want to hear the Word of God today. Perhaps (kumbure) we shall not receive another opportunity.
  6. I don't like your hat; can't you get another?
  7. You (s.) can pay five workmen today; you will pay the others another month.
  8. Find (look for) another cat, because we have a lot of rats.
  9. The teacher is going to Bujumbura to buy (some) more song books (= books of songs).
  10. The shepherd has 99 sheep, but he is looking for the other one.
