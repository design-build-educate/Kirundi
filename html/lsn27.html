LESSON 27: Class 8 ubu- ama-

Vocabulary

  • ubgato - boat
  • ubushaza - peas (or, ubjishaza) (no pl.)
  • uburiri - bed
  • ubgenge - wisdom (no pl.)
  • ubuntu - grace (no pl.)
  • ubugingo - life (no pl.)
  • kuko -because

50. Class 8.

Sing. Plural
Noun prefix ubu- ama-
e.g. uburiri amariri
Poss. adjective bga- ya-
e.g. bganje yanje
Verb prefix bu- a-
e.g. buri ari
Adj. prefix bu- ma-
e.g. bubi mabi

51. Most words in this cLaas have no pluraL. Ubgato and uburiri have plurals - amato, amariri: however, in speaking of a definite number of boats or beds one would use the singular form, ubgato, uburiri. The plural is used only when speaking of an indefinite number.

  • e.g. "ubgato bubiri - two boats" and "amato menshi - many boats"

52. The great majority of words in this class are those expressing abstract ideas, such as the last three nouns of the vocabulary, and they seldom have a plural form.

53. Perhaps this form looks a bit queer to you. Remember that u before a vowel becomes w; thus it becomes bwa, but since w after a b is pronounced like g, it is written bg. Have an African help with the pronunciation of this sound. Also notice the application of this same rule in certain adjectives: bgiza, bginshi. (In some non-Protestant publications this is written bwa, bwiza, etc.)

Note: "kuko - because" introduces a dependent clause. Remember rules about verb tenses in dependent clauses. Kuko and other conjunctions ending in -ko (like ariko) change ko to kw before words beginning with a,e, or i, and to k' before u.

Exercises:

I. Translate into English:

  1. Umwigisha mwiza afise ubgenge bginshi.
  2. Amato menshi cane agenda mu ruzi.
  3. Uburiri bg'umutunzi ni burebure cane.
  4. Nshima Imana kukw ifise ubuntu bginshi.
  5. Mfise ubugingo busha muri Yesu. (Translate muri as mu)
  6. Turakunda kurima ubushaza mu mirima yacu.
  7. Paulo n'umwigisa mwiza kukw afise ubgenge bginshi.
  8. Dusoma ivy' (about) ubuntu bga Yesu mu Gitabo c'Imana.
  9. Urukundo rw'Imana ruzana umuco mu mitima yacu.

II. Translate into Kirundi:

  1. The women have a few good peas.
  2. God's wisdom is very great (much)
  3. The men are going in boats.
  4. Do you (pl.) have the grace of Jesus in your hearts?
  5. Bring the short bed into the house.
  6. A wise person (a person of wisdom) loves God because He brings salvation into our hearts.
  7. God's mercy and grace bring peace into my life.
  8. Do you (pl.) see the boat on (mu) the river over there?
  9. The poor man wants to buy a new bed, but he hasn't (any) francs.
  10. I have joy because God loves to help people.
