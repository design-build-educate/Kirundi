LESSON 5: Mu and Ku

Vocabulary

  • umwōtsi - smoke
  • umwīza - darkness
  • umwēnda - debt
  • umuco - light
  • umūsi - day
  • umunēzēro - joy

9. Vowel change. You will note that the change of u to w before a vowel is the same as in first class. Also, I before another vowel becomes y. (Exceptions shown later.)

e.g. umu-enda becomes umwenda. imi-enda becomes imyenda.

10. Mu and Ku. These two prepositions have a number of uses, but for the present we Will learn only one:

  • mu - in, into, out of (the inside of a thing)
  • ku - on, at, to (the outside of a thing), from (outside of thing)

These various meanings are often dependent on the verb accompanying them.

  • Agenda mu mwiza - He is walking in the dark.
  • Thus: kuva - to come from
  • kuva mu muriro - to come out of the fire
  • kuva ku muriro - to come away from the fire

Note: Mu and ku usually remove the initial vowel of the noun following: not ku umuriro, but ku muriro. (For a variation of this see Par. 32.)

11. When verbs beginning with "ara-" or "nda"_(as in the following lesson) are followed by a phrase or object, other than cane, or ati, the "-ra-" or "da" usually drops out. This also applies to verbs in the other persons. (See Lesson 25.) e.g. Agenda mu mwiza (not, aragenda mu mwiza) - He goes in the dark.

I. Translate into English:

  1. Umugabo agenda mu mwiza.
  2. Umwigisha afise imyenda.
  3. Umugore afise umwenezero mu mutima wiwe.
  4. Abana biwe barakora neza.
  5. Umwotsi uva (comes from) mu muriro.
  6. Umutama afise umwiza mu mutima wiwe.
  7. Umukiza akora mu mitima yacu.
  8. Mbeg' umukozi afise imyenda? Oyaye.
  9. Abakozi barima ku musozi.

  4. Your(s.) debts.
  5. Our fire.
  6. In the fire.
  7. At the fire.
  8. The old men have darkness in their hearts.
  9. The light (insert "wo") on the hill is your fire.
