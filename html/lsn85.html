LESSON 85: Passives of Monosyllabic Verbs

Vocabulary

  • icubahiro - respect, reverence
  • ubgiza - glory, beauty
  • ijuru - sky, heaven
  • inzoga - beer
  • urusyo - grindstone (for millet, etc.)
  • ikiyoba - peanut, ground nut

169. The monosyllabic verbs form their passives in -bga. In the past forms the b drops out and the g becomes w. Learn these forms:

Infinitive Pres. Pass. Past Pass.
guca gucibga yaciwe
kugwa kugubga yaguwe
guha guhabga yahawe
kunywa kunyobga yanyowe
gusya gusebga yasewe
guta gutabga yatawe
kuva kuvubga yavuwe
kurya kuribga yariwe

170. Some of these passives could not be translated literally into English, but have an idiomatic use, such as kugubga and kuvubga:

  • kugubga means "to be settled down nicely" (yaguwe neza), or "to have good pleasant living".
  • Kugubga nabi would be the opposite. Kuvubga means "to be bereaved" - but isn't used a great deal.

171. Guhabga is not "to be given" in the sense of "the book was given to me", but nahawe igitabo - I was given a book. Thus, guhabga really means "to receive". It is often used for the future of -fise.

Exercises:

I.Translate into English

  1. Imana irahabga icubahiro mu gusenga kwacu.

  2. Inzoga nyinshi zanyowe n'abantu ku musi mukuru.

  3. Uburo bgasewe n'urusyo.

  4. Twebge tuzohabga ubgiza mw ijuru.

  5. Inyama ziri hehe? Zariwe n'akayabo.

  6. Umugore afise agahinda kenshi kuko yavuwe n'umwana mw iyinga reheze.

  7. Ndanezerewe ko turi hano i Gitega; numva ko twaguwe neza.

  8. Nabonye yuk' umushitsi yaje kubaha ibintu. Mbega mwaahawe iki?

  9. Ngomba gucumba umukate w'ibitoke; ndakoresha ibiyoba (insert "which") vyasewe.

  10. Dufise inkwi nkeya cane. Mbeg' ibindi biti bizocibga ryari?

II. Translate into Kirundi

  1. The clothes were thrown away because they were torn.

  2. Two trees were cut down by the men, but the fundi didn't want them.

  3. Let us give reverence (to) God for (because) He should (ikwiye followed by inf.) be given it.

  4. I want to go to (mu) heaven for we shall see Jesus and we shall have much joy.

  5. Much milk was drunk by the children.

  6. Is the meat of the sheep eaten by the Barundi? No, they don't eat it.

  7. Were my papers thrown away? Go, please look for them, because I want them.

  8. Jesus left his glory in heaven and came to earth. Here He was hated by many people.

  9. All the beer was poured out when (igihe) the people were saved.

  10. The good bricks and tiles were chosen by the builder, the others were thrown away.
